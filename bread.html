Sourdough Bread

Ingredients

  • 125 grams active sourdough starter
  • 325 grams warm filtered water
  • 12 grams extra virgin olive oil
  • 120 grams unbleached flour (all-purpose or bread flour)
  • 12 grams salt
  • Butter or cooking spray (for greasing the loaf pan)

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the active sourdough starter and warm filtered water. Mix well using a bread whisk or fork until most of the starter is dissolved.
  2. Add extra virgin olive oil and honey (or sugar) to the mixture. Stir until evenly incorporated.
  3. Gradually add unbleached flour to the mixture, followed by salt. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough in the bowl for about 3 minutes until it comes together and reaches a consistent texture.
  5. Cover the bowl with a kitchen towel and let the dough rest for 30 minutes.
  6. After 30 minutes, perform a series of stretch and folds on the dough. Repeat this process three more times, with 30 minutes of rest in between each set.
  7. Once the dough has almost doubled in size (about 3-4 hours), transfer it to a lightly floured surface.
  8. Shape the dough into a large rectangle. Use a lightly floured rolling pin to flatten the dough, working out all of the air bubbles.
  9. Fold one side of the dough toward the middle, and fold the other side to meet the first side in the middle. Roll the dough tightly into a loaf shape. Pinch and tuck the sides under.
  10. Place the dough seam side down in a greased 9x5 inch loaf pan.
  11. Cover the loaf pan with a kitchen towel and let the dough proof for 2 hours, or until it has almost doubled in size and fills the loaf pan.
  12. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) during the last 30 minutes of proofing.
  13. Once fully proofed, place the loaf pan in the preheated oven and bake for 35 minutes, or until the bread is golden brown.
  14. Remove the bread from the oven and immediately brush the top with butter. Allow the bread to cool in the pan for a few minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing. Enjoy!
